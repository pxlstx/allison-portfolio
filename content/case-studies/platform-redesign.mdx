## Overview

A legacy admin console was slowing down power users and blocking new feature adoption. I partnered with product and engineering to rethink the core workflow from first principles.

## Challenge

Operators spent most of their day in the tool, but navigation was inconsistent and key actions were buried three levels deep. Support tickets clustered around the same handful of usability gaps.

## Approach

We ran contextual inquiry with eight operators, mapped the critical path for daily tasks, and prototyped a simplified information architecture in Figma. Weekly design critiques with engineering kept scope honest.

## Outcome

- 34% faster task completion on the primary workflow
- 22% reduction in support tickets tied to navigation
- A scalable component library adopted across three product surfaces

## What I learned

Shipping incrementally behind feature flags let us validate IA changes without a big-bang release. The best feedback came from watching people work, not from preference tests alone.
